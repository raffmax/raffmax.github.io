I contributed to this course at the University of Stuttgart with Remco Leine as the main lecturer. The teaching overview notes a large cohort and identifies elastostatics and Euler-Bernoulli beam theory among the covered topics.

## Course Description

Engineering Mechanics II, taught in German as `Technische Mechanik II`, focuses on elastostatics and the relation between external loads, internal stresses, and deformation. Starting from axially deformable bars, the course develops stress and strain into full tensor descriptions, derives local equilibrium equations from virtual work and variational principles, and concludes with Euler-Bernoulli beam theory, including bending, torsion, axial deformation, and shear stresses.

## Details

- German title: `Technische Mechanik II`
- Main lecturer: Remco Leine
- Institution: University of Stuttgart
- Typical cohort size: about 200 students

## Examination Work

The teaching overview also records written-examination support for related mechanics courses:

- `Technische Mechanik II (TM 2)`: `1` written examination. The course focuses on elastostatics, stress and strain tensors, variational formulations, and Euler-Bernoulli beam theory.
- `Mechanik 2`: `5` written examinations. This follow-on bachelor's course builds on Mechanics 1 and covers the statics of linear continua, beam theory, planar rigid-body dynamics, momentum and energy balances, and linear vibrations with one degree of freedom.
