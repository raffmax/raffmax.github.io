This course was offered at the University of Stuttgart with André Schmidt as the main lecturer. The teaching overview records a small master's-level cohort and highlights my contribution to structured in-class problem solving.

## Course Description

This course introduces the finite element method as a core engineering tool for the approximate solution of complex problems. It focuses on reliable FEM formulations for statics and dynamics, while also touching applications such as heat transfer. Topics include weighted residuals, virtual work, element matrices for bars, beams, and higher-order elements, shape functions, assembly of the global system, numerical integration, eigenvalue problems, and time integration.
