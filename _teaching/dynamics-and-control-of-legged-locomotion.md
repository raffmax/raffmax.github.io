This master's course was taught at the University of Stuttgart with C. David Remy as the main lecturer. I contributed to the development of lecture content and prepared three exercises for the first half of the course on legged-locomotion dynamics.

## Course Description

This course provides an overview of the state of the art in the dynamics and control of legged robotic systems. Using legged locomotion as a concrete application area, it covers biomechanics and locomotion in nature, multibody and nonsmooth dynamics, nonlinear dynamics, limit cycles, continuation and bifurcation, as well as modern control and optimal-control strategies. Hands-on programming exercises and a robotic locomotion competition connect the theory to practice.
