This page presents ongoing work on the co-design of legged systems, with a focus on how natural dynamics can guide both morphology and behavior. The interactive multimedia content below complements the introduction video by highlighting different aspects of how a system's morphology shapes its motion space.

Compass-Gait Walker

The compass-gait walker is one of the simplest models of legged locomotion. It has four degrees of freedom and is characterized by two design parameters: the leg mass m and the leg center-of-mass a. After normalization by leg length l0, total mass m0, and gravity g0, the remaining parameters follow from the constraints a + b = l0 and 2m + M = m0.

Feel free to change the design parameters and explore how the motion changes from standstill to downhill passive walking as the slope angle γ is varied.
