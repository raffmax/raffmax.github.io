My research in robotics focuses on the interplay between mechanics, dynamics, and control. I am particularly interested in legged systems, where morphology, passive behavior, and algorithmic decision-making need to be designed together to achieve robust and efficient motion.

The publications below can be explored through four recurring themes:

Optimization covers methods for trajectory generation and gait design.

Natural Dynamics highlights how passive mechanics shape motion.

Co-Design brings hardware and behavior optimization together.

Learning collects work that connects dynamical systems with data-driven methods.
